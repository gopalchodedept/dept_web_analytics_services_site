This page is for anyone client-facing: growth, client partners, account leads. It covers how to spot an analytics opportunity in a client conversation, who to bring it to, and how it moves from a hallway mention to a staffed, kicked-off project.

Step zero

What To
Listen For

You don't need to know GA4 from a hole in the ground. You just need to recognize these sentences when a client says them. Each one maps directly to work we sell.

S1

We're redesigning our website / moving to a new CMS.

Website Migration

The biggest one. Every redesign breaks tracking by default, and most clients find out after launch. If you hear this, we should already be in the room.

The migration playbook →
S2

We don't trust our numbers.

GA4 Audit

Mismatched reports, conversions that look wrong, dashboards nobody believes. A fixed-scope audit finds every broken, duplicated, or missing tag and prioritizes the fixes.

What the audit covers →
S3

We're launching campaigns and need conversion tracking.

Marketing Pixels

Meta, Google Ads, Floodlight, LinkedIn, TikTok. We implement and QA the pixels through GTM so ad spend is measured accurately from day one.

The Google stack →
S4

We have Adobe licenses / we're on AEP.

The Adobe Stack

Enterprise clients with Adobe entitlements are sitting on capability they usually haven't activated: Web SDK, CJA, Real-Time CDP, AJO. We implement it end to end.

The Adobe practice →

Measurement Framework

KPI identification for the business: what matters, how each metric is calculated, who owns it. The strategic engagement that makes everything else coherent.

From signal to kickoff

How A Deal
Moves

Eight steps, same order every time. Most of the work sits with us; your part is steps one, two, and five.

01
Step 01 · You

Spot The Signal

A client says one of the sentences above, mentions a redesign, complains about their data, or asks what to do with their Adobe licenses. That's the moment. You don't need to qualify it; you just need to not let it pass.

Your move → Note what they said and who said it
02
Step 02 · You

Reach Out To Gopal Or Saj

Message Gopal Chode (Web Analytics Lead) or Saj Patel (VP, Head of Analytics, Americas). We'll jump on a call with you, dig into what the client actually needs, and tell you straight whether there's a real opportunity here.

What to bring
  • What the client said, in their words
  • What platform they're on today, if you know (GA4, Adobe, neither)
  • Any timeline pressure, like a site launch date
Output → A shared view of the potential opportunity
03
Step 03 · Us, with you

Proposal Deck (If Needed)

For bigger or less-defined opportunities, we build a proposal deck for the client: the problem as we see it, the approach, what they get. Then we walk the client through it together, you on the relationship, us on the substance.

Output → A proposal the client can react to
04
Step 04 · Us

Estimates & SOW Draft

Once the client agrees to the direction, we produce the numbers and the paper: a clear estimate and a drafted SOW. We do the heavy lifting on the draft, covering scope, deliverables, and everything the engagement includes.

What's in it
  • Timeline, phase by phase
  • Roles required and level of effort
  • Budget
  • Scope and deliverables, spelled out
Output → Estimate + SOW draft, ready for the client
05
Step 05 · You

Heads-Up To Sari & Nico

Before anything goes to the client, loop in our resource managers. This is your responsibility as the person bringing the deal. Data work goes to Sari; Adobe work goes to both Sari and Nico. At this stage it's just a heads-up: something may be coming, roughly this shape, roughly this timing.

Your move → One message to Sari and Nico. That's it.
06
Step 06 · You, with us

Send To The Client

The estimate and SOW go to the client through you. We stay on hand for any follow-up questions, scope adjustments, or a walkthrough call if the client wants one.

Output → Estimate + SOW in the client's hands
07
Step 07 · Us, with the RMs

Signed → Staff It

Once the client approves, we work with the resource managers, Sari for data work and Sari plus Nico for Adobe work, to lock the right people for the project: the roles from the SOW, matched to real names, confirmed against the start date.

The formal step
  • Submit a resourcing ticket for the project and get it approved
  • The ticket is what turns the earlier heads-up into an actual staffing request
  • First time doing this? Work through it with Sari or Nico, they'll walk you through the ticket and the approval
Output → An approved resourcing ticket, a staffed team, and a confirmed start date
08
Step 08 · Everyone

Kickoff

Aligned start date, resourced team, signed scope. From here the engagement runs on the playbooks documented across this site, and you get a client whose analytics actually work.

Output → Project underway
