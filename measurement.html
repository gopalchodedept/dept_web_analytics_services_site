Practice, strategy

Measurement
Framework.

Before a single tag is written, someone has to decide what the business is actually trying to prove. A measurement framework identifies the KPIs that matter, defines exactly how each one is calculated, and assigns every metric an owner, so reporting starts with answers instead of exports.

The philosophy

"Measurement is how we prove an experience doesn't just drive engagement; it moves the business."

// activity metrics are useful as diagnostic indicators, but they are never the story. Outcomes are the story.

The core distinction

Outcomes
Over Activity

Activity metrics

Page views. Sessions. Impressions. Clicks.

Easy to produce. Easy to present. Useful as diagnostic indicators, but they only tell you the engine is on, not whether it is taking you anywhere.

Outcomes

A booked meeting. A qualified inquiry. A returning account. Pipeline created.

The moments that mean the business actually moved. Every KPI in the framework ladders up to one of these, and anything that doesn't gets cut.

The rules we hold ourselves to

Five Principles

01

Signal Over Noise

We track fewer things better, not everything poorly. If a metric is noisy, we cut it.

02

Actionability Is Required

If a metric does not inform a decision, it is not in the framework. Full stop.

03

Tiered By Readiness

We separate what is measurable today (MVP) from what unlocks post-launch, so nothing blocks shipping.

04

Owned, Not Shared

Every KPI has a designated owner who responds when something is off. Shared ownership is no ownership.

05

Evolving By Design

The framework is reviewed quarterly. What matters in Q1 may not be what matters in Q3.

KPI architecture

Every KPI,
Fully Specified

A KPI that lives in a slide title is a wish. In our framework, every KPI is documented across six dimensions, deep enough that an implementation team can build it without a single follow-up meeting.

KPI + why it matters

The Point

What we are tracking and why it connects to a business or program goal. If this line is weak, the KPI doesn't make the cut.

Building blocks

The Raw Inputs

The inputs the system must capture to calculate the KPI. These feed straight into data layer specs and XDM field mappings.

KPI calculation

The Formula

The exact formula that turns building blocks into a single, comparable KPI value. No ambiguity, no two teams computing it differently.

Reporting metrics

The Outputs

The derived views teams read and act on, segmented by platform, topic, persona, account tier, or whatever the business slices by.

Business owner

The Accountable Team

Who monitors this KPI and responds when something is off. Named before launch, not after the first bad week.

Readiness

MVP Or Post-Launch

Whether the KPI is measurable at MVP with current tools, or requires an integration that lands after launch.

What it looks like in practice

A Worked Example

One KPI from a brand-authority framework, documented to the standard above. Every KPI we ship gets this treatment.

AI Citation Rate (Brand Visibility Score)

Answers "is the AI mentioning the brand when a user asks a relevant industry question?", the modern equivalent of a page-one search ranking.

MVP · Marketing Ops
Building blocks
  • Pre-defined industry and intent-relevant prompts (30 to 100)
  • Total brand citations in LLM responses
  • Total relevant prompts tested
KPI calculation

Citation Rate % =
Brand Citations /
Prompts Tested × 100

Reporting metrics
  • Citation rate by LLM platform
  • Citation rate by topic cluster
  • Citation position (first vs later)
Readiness

Measurable at MVP with current tooling. Owner monitors weekly; reviewed with the rest of the framework quarterly.
